2. Cross-Account Prevention & Defence Factors

Prevention FactorWhat It Defends AgainstConcrete Examples
Measurement & infra hygiene / risk-mitigation Sudden “performance drops” that are really data issues, which would otherwise trigger budget cuts. CenturyGames: Fixed LAT revenue under-prediction, supply restrictions, attribution leakage, RMG mis-classification, and TCM/CPI oscillation before they turned into “Moloco is broken” stories. Effect: protects a very large baseline DRR while we test T2/T3/RU and new titles.
Framing lifecycle & channel roles clearly Being blamed when natural lifecycle / channel shifts (e.g. Smartdex/ASA ramp) reduce Moloco share. Winner / Luckymoney: Position Moloco as strongest in launch + GP phases; Smartdex/ASA as designed for very large, mature budgets. Prevents mis-framing “Moloco underperforming” when share shrinks after Smartdex ramps.
Blueprints / playbooks for “safe” campaign structures Ad-hoc setups causing TCM oscillation, CPI spikes, under-spend, which then force conservative budget decisions. Century blueprint: Consolidation + overlap rules for P0 structure.
Aviagames / Solitaire Clash: Image-heavy tKPI + CPI balancer + D1/D3 + traffic control as safe default config.
Wedobest: KR/JP/TW → region → WW geo ladder as default rollout.
Combined, these give GMs default safe templates so new tests don’t blow up.
Guardrails on creative & geo changes Over-aggressive creative/geo tests that tank short-term metrics and justify cutting Moloco budgets. Wedobest: Move to normal creatives with clear before/after data. Enforce KR/JP/TW → region → WW ladder so teams can’t say “we tried WW once and it failed, so no more tests”.
Policy / filter diagnostics before scale decisions Clients keeping or re-introducing over-strict filters that starve good supply and make Moloco look weak. Aviagames: Showing top-publisher ROI + creative mix (images ~50%+ of spend on best pubs) proved VT + CPM<1 rules blocked high-value supply. Once filters were removed and monitored, risk of BI reverting them is lower.
Codifying “what good looks like” early (reference curves) New launches being judged too harshly or too late due to lack of benchmarks. Use early-phase DRR & KPI curves from Ten Luck, MU, Solitaire Clash UK, WoolCrush (~7k DRR) as reference launch patterns. If a new title underperforms vs these curves → debug. If it matches/beats them → evidence to argue for more budget, not less.
