Weekly Update — March 16, 2026

Spend Summary

Non-IAA overall

  • Non-IAA grew +2.5% W/W (+$467k).
  • Growth was led by Gaming: +4.4% W/W (+$893k).
    • Netmarble (KOR): +$631k from a new title launch, contributing ~70% of Gaming uplift.
    • Madhouse (CHN, "Last Asylum"): +$264k, contributing the remaining ~30%.

By OS

  • Android: +2.5% W/W (+$467k), fully driven by Netmarble (+$529k).
  • iOS: +1.6% W/W (+$436k), primarily driven by MFG – Google Gemini (+$397k).

IAA overall

  • IAA (including hybrid IAA/IAP) grew +4.2% W/W (+$551k).
  • Growth was entirely driven by non‑Hungry Studio advertisers: +$1.0m W/W (+13.6%), offsetting a Hungry Studio pullback of −$453k (−7.7%), as Hungry reverted to its $5.3m–$5.5m baseline from the past 5 weeks after last week’s spike.
  • Weekly IAA incremental growth was broad-based:
    • Goodroid (JPN): +$191k, contributing roughly one-third of the weekly IAA uplift.
    • Lessmore (EMEA): +$181k, contributing roughly another one-third.
    • Bravestar (SGP): +$90k.
    • Madhouse (CHN): +$85k.
  • IAA growth is broadening across the advertiser base:
    • Active IAA apps increased +11% W/W to 585 apps (+58).
    • Apps averaging $1k+/day in IAA spend increased +7% W/W to 218 apps (+14).
